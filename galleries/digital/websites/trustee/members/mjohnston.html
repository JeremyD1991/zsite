Mark Johnston

President and CEO of Community Journals

Mark B. Johnston is president, CEO and co-founder of Community Journals. After graduation from the University of Kansas School of Journalism, Mr. Johnston began his newspaper career in 1982 at the Kansas City Star as a business product manager and later as a retail advertising manager. He left the Kansas City Star in 1992 to become director of marketing with Suburban Journals of Greater St. Louis, a community weekly operation which then published 47 local editions with a weekly circulation of 950,000. He then served as the vice president of advertising at The Greenville News, a position he held from April 1994 to January 1999. In his role at the News, Mr. Johnston was responsible for all revenue operations for the company as well as the introduction and roll out of the newspapers fist digital offering, www.greenvilleonline. Mr. Johnston left the News in 1999 to develop the current metro-wide weekly Journal model and the successful execution of that model (both in print and digital), the Greenville Journal, in the Upstate of South Carolina. The Greenville Journal is now one of the largest, privately owned, community weekly newspapers and digital content distribution platforms in the country with a weekly circulation of 35,000.

Mr. Johnston was also responsible for the concept, design, and execution of the Companyâ€™s successful magazine group launching the nationally recognized At Home magazine brand in 2002 and the introduction the upscale lifestyle magazine TOWN in 2009. Following the highly successful introduction of TOWN magazine, Mr. Johnston led the development and execution of the Company's regional business magazine, the Upstate Business Journal, in 2013.

Currently all Journal brands have attendant digital products of either web sites, targeted emails or both. Most recently Mr. Johnston was responsible for the execution and revenue generation of 6am City LLC, the Company's innovative, local, aggregated content distribution model with a growing digital presence in many markets in the Southeast.

Mr. Johnston has participated on numerous community boards including the Upcountry History Museum, Centre Stage, Metropolitan Arts Council, the Greenville Chamber of Commerce, the Boy Scouts of America, and the Greenville Symphony Orchestra.

Mr. Johnston has held the position of president and chief executive officer of Community Journals since its inception in February of 1999. He received his MBA in 1991.
