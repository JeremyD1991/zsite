  Patton Oswalt
Sat, March 30 • 8 PM • Peace Concert Hall • $45-$75
A comedian, actor, and writer, Patton Oswalt continues to find success in all areas of entertainment. As a comedian, Oswalt has shot seven TV specials and released five critically-acclaimed albums. Last year, he won the Emmy® Award for Talking for Clapping (Netflix) and a Grammy® Award for his comedy album of the same name. On TV, Oswalt is well known for playing Spence on The King of Queens for nine seasons, and has appeared in Caprica, United States of Tara, Veep, Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D., Parks and Recreation, Two and a Half Men, Portlandia, and Seinfeld, among others. He can be found in films such as The Secret Life of Walter Mitty, The Informant, Zoolander, Starsky and Hutch, and Reno 911!: Miami.

  Jeanne Robertson
Fri, May 17 • 7:30 PM • Peace Concert Hall • $35-$55
At 75 years young, Jeanne Robertson continues to charm audiences with humorous observations about life around her. She has eight nationally released DVDs, three books, hundreds of hours on SiriusXM satellite radio, and over 56 million YouTube views. Some of her most popular anecdotes include "Don't Go to Vegas Without A Baptist," "Don't Bungee Jump Naked," and "Don't Send a Man to the Grocery Store." Standing tall at six-foot-two, this former Miss North Carolina never fails to have audiences of all ages rolling with laughter.
